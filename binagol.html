BINAGOL RECIPE

DESRCIPTION

Binagol is a Filipino sweet steamed delicacy of the Waray people
made from mashed giant taro corms, condensed milk, sugar, coconut
milk, and egg yolks. It is distinctively placed in half of a coconut
shell and then wrapped in banana leaves and twine.

INGREDIENTS

- Taro
- ½ cup Glutinous rice flour
- 1 cup Condensed milk

For filling :
- 1 cup Coconut milk
- ½ cup Crushed peanuts
- ¼ cup Butter
- 1 cup Sugar

For wrapping :
- Coconut’s nut or shell
- Banana leaves
- Thin string

PROCEDURE

1. Scrape the taro.
2. Mix it with condensed milk, and glutinous rice flour.
3. Set aside, and let's make for the filling.
4. Mix coconut milk, crushed peanuts, butter, and sugar in a pan.
5. Stir it into low heat until it becomes smooth.
6. Ready the coconut shell (bilao) for wrapping.
7. Fill the coconut shell with filling first.
8. Add the mix texture around the bilao.
9. Cover coconut shell using banana leaves and tie it using string securely.
10. Steam for about an hour or to 8 hours.
11. Ready to serve, enjoy your binagol!

FACTS

Binagol is known to be sweet because of the latik which complements
the plain taste of the Talyan mixture. It is wrapped in banana leaves
and tied tightly with a string to secure its contents from spilling out.
Try this savory sweet delicacy that you will surely love, as well as your family.
