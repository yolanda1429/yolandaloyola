NILUPAK RECIPE

DESRCIPTION

Nilupak is a class of traditional Filipino delicacies made from mashed or pounded starchy
foods mixed with coconut milk (or condensed milk and butter) and sugar. They are molded
into various shapes and traditionally served on banana leaves with toppings of grated
young coconut (buko), various nuts, cheese, butter, or margarine. It is also known as
nilusak, linusak, niyubak, linupak, or lubi-lubi, among many other names, in the various
languages of the Philippines. It is also known as minukmok in Quezon.

INGREDIENTS

- Cassava
- 1/4 cup Condensed milk
- 1/4 Margarine

For toppings :
- Grated cheese

For packaging :
- Clam shell
- Banana leaves

PROCEDURE

1. Wash the cassava and peel it.
2. Steam for 40 minutes.
3. Scrape or mash the cassava.
4. Add evaporared milk, and margarine.
5. Mix it until it becomes smooth.
6. Ready the clam shell and banana leaves for wrapping.
7. Add the mix texture around the clam shell with banana leaves.
8. Add grated cheese for toppings.
9. Ready to serve, enjoy your nilupak!

FACTS

Nilupak is a class of traditional Filipino delicacies made from mashed or
pounded starchy foods mixed with coconut milk and sugar. They are molded
